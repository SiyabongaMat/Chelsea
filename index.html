The Bridge

                The Stamford Bridge, football stadium in Fulham, South West London
                Home to Chelsea football club with the capacity to hold 41 837 fans
                within the stadium. It also goes by the nickname THE BRIDGE
                The stadium first opened in 1877 and used by London Athletic Club 
                until Gus Mears founded Chelsea and used the stadium as home for 
                his newfoundclub. Chelsea has played their home games there since 
                and has undergone changes with the most recent change done in the
                1990s and was changed into the now modern all-seater stadium.
                
                Over the years the stadium has hosted various events, including:

                    England international games
                    FA cup finals
                    FA cup semifinals
                    Community shield games

Cobham Training Ground

                    The Cobham is the training ground for Chelsea F.C in the village 
                    of Stoke d'Abernon. The first team has been training in the 
                    Cobham since 2005 but wasn't officially open until 2007.
                    Worth 20 million pounds, the training ground is situated in a 
                    140-acre ground which houses all the fooball clubs activities,
                    from the 1st team, reserve team, academy and women's team.
                    It includes:       
                                30 football fields
                                Indoor fields
                                Media room
                                Sauna
                                Gyms
                                Steam room etc,
